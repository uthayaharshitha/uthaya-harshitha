PSYCHOLOGY + UX / INTERACTION DESIGN

DESIGNING FOR PRESENCE IN SPACES BUILT FOR DISTRACTION.

I'm Uthaya Harshitha a psych undergrad interested in creating intuitive, reflective and meaningful digital experiences.

My current work uses psychological concepts to influence design decisions across experimental and interactive projects. I'm particularly interested in expanding into different facets of UX/UI and developing a deeper understanding of how design influences user experience.

Explore my works and feel free to connect for further discussions and collaborations.

Location Chennai, India
Project 01

THE
VESTIBULE

A web platform for open-ended expression outside fast, feed-based interaction models. Users create "capsules" that combine text, audio, and visuals, without metrics or visibility-driven feedback. Interaction is slowed and non-linear, allowing exploration without performance pressure. The space is shaped by an eerie, Liminal atmosphere that reinforces reflection and ambiguity.

An interaction system grounded in the online disinhibition effect, translating behavioral theory into interface design. It introduces real-time feedback mechanisms such as visibility shifts, audience presence cues, and identity-based consequences. Rather than relying solely on traditional moderation, the system embeds behavioral awareness directly into interaction. Actions accumulate and influence how users are perceived within the environment.

SECOND
SKIN

A fashion interface built around context rather than aesthetics. Instead of browsing abstract styles, users navigate clothing through real-life situations—social settings, expectations, and environments. The system reduces ambiguity by translating "what should I wear here?" into structured, visual guidance. It is especially oriented toward users who struggle with implicit social cues.

Academic Research 05

ONLINE
DISINHIBITION
EFFECT

Conducted a quantitative study to examine the impact of the Online Disinhibition Effect on online deviant behavior. Findings revealed a strong positive relationship between online disinhibition and deviant behavior, indicating that reduced social presence significantly increases toxic interactions.

science Quantitative Research

Impact of Online Disinhibition on Online Deviant Behaviour

The research focused on how factors such as anonymity, reduced social cues, and lowered accountability influence behaviors like hostility, aggression, and norm violations in digital environments.

design_services Design Translation

From Research to Interface

These findings later informed the development of a moderation-based UI prototype, translating behavioral insights into design interventions aimed at reducing toxic online behavior — directly linking to the LOOM project.
